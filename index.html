Jiannan Shi 石剑南
conceiving public spaces for future human society

Jiannan Shi is a civic engagement and media advocate and human-centered researcher . Currently, Jiannan is studying how technology interacts with human society at an MPhil student in the Department of Sociology, University of Cambridge. A Humanity in Action Senior Fellow (Berlin, Germany) and previously a PEER Fellow (Hunan, China), Jiannan facilitated media literacy education for teens in rural high schools. Jiannan graduated magna cum laude from a revolutionary education experiment – New York University (NYU) Shanghai, with an honors Bachelor's degree in Social Science and two minors in Interactive Media Arts and Journalism from NYU's Global Campuses.

Their education begins and finds root in China, spaning across the US, Czech Republic, Germany, and the UK. Mirroring their global adventure, Jiannan always likes to explore noodle restaurants in town whenever they travel – savoring the culinary nuances that make these cosmopolitan carbohydrates-based thin strips distinct in their ways.

Amidst the differences, what captivates Jiannan is how these noodles across the world can evoke a taste of childhood, reminiscent of those made by the bank of the lower yellow river where Jiannan calls home.
