Nihao! I'm Jiannan. I currently live and study in Cambridge, UK. People often call me Nan, if it's easier to pronounce.

On the academic side, I investigate how technology can better foster future public spaces and civil society. To understand "public spaces," I had ethnographies on wet markets, queer spaces, and social media sites in the digital world.

I am a Humanity in Action Senior Fellow and previously a PEER Fellow, I curated and managed public spaces in rural high schools in China and initiated media literacy education programs for teens.

Much fortune and privilege has shaped me on a global path of education, from Shandong to Shanghai, Prague to New York, and now in Cambridge.

Mirroring my global adventure, I always like to explore noodle restaurants in town whenever I travel — savoring the culinary nuances that make these cosmopolitan carbohydrates-based thin strips distinct in their ways.

Amidst the differences, what captivates me is how these noodles across the world can evoke a taste of childhood, reminiscent of those made by the bank of the lower yellow river where I would still call home.
